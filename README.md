# Effects of Sleep Deprivation on Young, Healthy Males

## Abstract
Sleep deprivation is classified as getting < 7 hours of sleep and is known to have many impacts on physical and neural functions. The purpose of this study is to explore the factors that define sleep deprivation, and exploring if sleep deprivation can be predicted based on different neural measurements. The study includes 9 males aged 18-65 who are assessed before and after sleep deprivation. This study observes the fNIRS brain signals and cognitive test results to construct a classification model for sleep deprivation prediction. The model is shown to be accurate and successful in predicting the presence of sleep deprivation vs no sleep deprivation.

## Background
Data was collected from https://physionet.org/content/sleep-dep-hemo-cog/1.0.0/. The study protocol was approved by the Institutional Review Board of the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center and was conducted in compliance with the Helsinki Declaration. Data was extracted from the provided files to create a machine learning model. Not all files were used in this project.
